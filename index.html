About Me

From curious beginner to deliberate craftsman.

I started coding in 2022 with nothing but curiosity and a slow laptop. What began as "how do websites even work?" turned into late nights with HTML and CSS, then JavaScript, then Bootstrap — and a growing obsession with making things feel right, not just look right.

Today, my workspace is simple: dual monitors, a dark theme, a cup of coffee, and a desk lamp that glows the same cyan as my code editor. I care about clean markup, responsive layouts that hold up on any screen, and small interactions that make a site feel alive.

When I'm not building, I'm reading docs, breaking my own projects on purpose, or helping other beginners get unstuck — because that's exactly how I got here.

Experience

My journey so far

From first lines of code to shipping real projects — one year at a time.

2022 — Getting Started

First lines of HTML & CSS

Began learning frontend development from scratch — building static pages, experimenting with layouts, and falling in love with the instant feedback of "view in browser."

2023 — Going Interactive

Learning JavaScript

Moved from static pages to interactive ones — DOM manipulation, small games, and form validation. Started thinking like a developer, not just a designer.

2024 — Real Projects

Mastered Bootstrap, shipped NaijaEats & ShopFront

Combined custom CSS with Bootstrap to ship faster without losing control over design. Built my first two real-world style projects: a food ordering UI and an e-commerce storefront.

2025 — Building for Others

Launched BootFlow UI Kit & DevBlog

Released a reusable component kit for other developers, started a dev blog, and took on my first freelance clients — learning to turn designs and feedback into polished code.

2026 — Present

Refining the craft, open to new challenges

Currently sharpening my JavaScript fundamentals, mentoring beginners in frontend communities, and looking for a team where I can grow as a developer while contributing from day one.
